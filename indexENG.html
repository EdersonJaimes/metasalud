Everyone Against Dengue

Dengue is a viral disease transmitted by mosquitoes, especially the Aedes aegypti. We can all help prevent it!

How to prevent Dengue?

  • Identify mosquito breeding sites and report them to local authorities.
  • Eliminate mosquito breeding sites, such as standing water in containers.
  • Use mosquito repellent and place mosquito nets on beds.
  • Wear clothing that covers most of your skin.
  • See a doctor if you have symptoms like high fever and muscle pain.
  • Eat healthily; maintaining healthy habits reduces the risk of mortality.

Diet and Nutrition Tips

Maintain a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet is key to good health. Be sure to include foods from all food groups:

  • Vegetables and greens (half of your plate should consist of them).
  • Lean proteins (chicken, fish, eggs, legumes).
  • Healthy carbohydrates (brown rice, oats, potatoes, quinoa).
  • Fresh fruits (consume at least 3 servings per day).

Stay Properly Hydrated

Water is essential for the proper functioning of your body. Try to drink at least 8 glasses of water a day, especially if you exercise or if the weather is hot.

Avoid Refined Sugars

Added sugars in products like sodas, cookies, and artificial juices can cause weight gain and affect metabolic health. Opt for natural fruits or foods without added sugar.

Incorporate Healthy Fats

Fats are not bad, but it’s important to choose the right ones. Opt for healthy fats found in avocado, nuts, olive oil, and fatty fish like salmon.

Don’t Skip Breakfast

Breakfast is the most important meal of the day. Make sure to include proteins and fiber, such as scrambled eggs with spinach or a bowl of oatmeal with fruits.

Control Portions

Eating controlled portions is key to avoid excess calories. Use smaller plates and listen to your body’s hunger cues.
